Scientific Programme — ISCC 2026

Bioethics and Drug Discovery in AI Technology

THEME 01

Fundamental Elements of AI in Drug R&D

Machine learning architectures, data ontologies, and computational modelling as the three foundational pillars of AI-driven pharmaceutical research. Examining transformer models, graph neural networks, and generative chemistry.

THEME 02

Applications of AI in Drug Discovery

Target identification, virtual screening, de novo molecular generation, ADMET prediction, and lead optimisation. Case studies from academia and biotech. Validation pipelines and wet-lab integration.

THEME 03

AI Applications Across the Pharmaceutical Industry

Clinical trial design optimisation, pharmacovigilance, real-world evidence mining, drug repurposing, manufacturing process control, and regulatory submission automation.

THEME 04

Challenges and Potential Solutions in AI for Drug R&D

Data scarcity, model interpretability, bias in training sets, regulatory acceptance, bioethical implications of autonomous decision-making, and reproducibility standards. Proposed governance frameworks.

Aims, Scope & Disciplines

What We Publish

Scientiae-Naturalis publishes original research articles, systematic reviews, methodological papers, and perspectives across the natural sciences, with a focus on computationally intensive and interdisciplinary work at the interface of biology, chemistry, physics, and data science.

Computational BiologyBioinformatics, structural biology, systems biology, network medicine.
AI & Machine LearningDrug discovery, clinical decision support, biomedical NLP, computer vision.

Author Guidelines — Technical Specifications

Manuscript Requirements

Word LimitsResearch article: 6,000–10,000 words. Review: up to 15,000 words. Letter: 1,500 words.
File FormatsManuscript: DOCX or LaTeX. Figures: TIFF / EPS ≥ 300 dpi. Supplementary: ZIP.
ReferencingVancouver style. DOI required where available. Max 60 references (original articles).
Data AvailabilityFAIR data principles mandatory. Raw datasets deposited in Zenodo, Figshare, or equivalent.
EthicsIRB/ethics board approval required for all human or animal studies. Statement in manuscript.
APC / Open AccessArticle Processing Charge: € 450 (standard). Waiver available for low-income country institutions.
Code AvailabilityReproducible code hosted on GitHub or similar. Archived version via Zenodo.
LanguageEnglish only. Professional language editing recommended. Grammarly or equivalent.
